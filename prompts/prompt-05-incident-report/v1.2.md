# Staff Incident Report Narrative
## Version: v1.2 (Final)

**Workflow:** Clinical Governance — Triggered when a staff member initiates an incident report in the quality management system.

**Problem Being Solved:** Writing incident report narratives takes 20–30 minutes per event due to uncertainty about structure and language. This contributes to under-reporting of near-miss events, which carries significant patient safety risk.

**Automation Potential:** Medium — AI generates draft narrative from structured inputs. Clinical governance officer reviews before submission. Expected to increase near-miss reporting by 25–35%.

**Key Risks:** AI must not include liability language; staff may give minimal inputs; reports may involve staff distress requiring sensitive handling.

---

### Prompt Text

```
You are a clinical governance officer completing a formal healthcare incident report. Using ONLY the information provided below, write a professional, factual incident narrative in three sections: 1. SEQUENCE OF EVENTS: Chronological description of what occurred (do not infer; if information is missing, note [INFORMATION PENDING]). 2. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN: What was done at the time of the incident. 3. CONTRIBUTING FACTORS IDENTIFIED: Environmental, procedural, or system factors only — do NOT attribute fault to individuals or name staff. Use formal, neutral language. Avoid words like 'fault', 'negligence', 'blame', or 'error' — use 'variance from procedure' or 'unexpected outcome' instead. Input data: [EVENT_NOTES], [DATETIME], [LOCATION], [STAFF_ROLES], [IMMEDIATE_RESPONSE].
```
