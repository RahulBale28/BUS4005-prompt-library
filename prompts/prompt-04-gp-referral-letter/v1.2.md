# GP Referral Letter
## Version: v1.2 (Final)

**Workflow:** Specialist Referral — Outpatient Step 2. Triggered when GP confirms specialist referral in practice management system.

**Problem Being Solved:** GPs spend 10–15 minutes drafting referral letters per patient. With 8 referrals/day average, this represents 80–120 minutes of physician time daily — estimated at $400–600/day per GP in lost consultation revenue.

**Automation Potential:** High — AI drafts clinical referral letter from structured consultation notes. GP reviews and edits in under 2 minutes.

**Key Risks:** Errors in referral letters can cause diagnostic delays; contains sensitive patient health information; AI may produce inappropriate register if not specified.

---

### Prompt Text

```
You are an experienced General Practitioner. Write a formal, professional specialist referral letter with the following structure: TO: [SPECIALIST_NAME/SPECIALIST_TYPE] | PATIENT: [PATIENT_NAME], DOB: [DOB], Medicare No: [MEDICARE] | REASON FOR REFERRAL: [CLINICAL_REASON — 2–3 sentences] | CLINICAL HISTORY: [HISTORY_NOTES — bullet points, chronological] | CURRENT MEDICATIONS: [MEDICATION_LIST — generic names, doses] | RELEVANT INVESTIGATIONS: [TEST_RESULTS] | SPECIFIC REQUEST: [What you are asking the specialist to assess or manage] | Signed: [GP_NAME], [CLINIC_NAME], [DATE]. Use formal clinical register. Do not speculate on diagnosis. Flag any time-sensitive items as URGENT.
```

### Iteration Record

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Change Made** | Full structured template with explicit section headers, URGENT flagging, prohibition on diagnostic speculation |
| **Observed Effect** | GP review time reduced from ~12 min to under 2 min; letter quality rated 4.6/5 by pilot GPs |
| **Lesson Learned** | Specifying 'do not speculate on diagnosis' is essential — AI otherwise adds differential diagnoses not requested |
