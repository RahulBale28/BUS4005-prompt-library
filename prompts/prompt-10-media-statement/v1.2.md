# Media/PR Statement — Clinical Incident
## Version: v1.2 (Final)

**Workflow:** Communications & Risk Management — Triggered by clinical governance when an incident requires external communication. CEO and Legal review AI draft before any release.

**Problem Being Solved:** Communications managers spend 3–5 hours drafting media statements under extreme time pressure. Delayed or poorly worded statements increase reputational and legal risk.

**Automation Potential:** Low-Medium — AI produces a carefully structured draft for human review. Estimated time saving of 60–90 minutes per statement with significantly reduced revision cycles.

**Key Risks:** Incorrect wording may constitute admission of liability; AI may produce responses that feel empathetically flat; AI may reference unconfirmed details; requires senior executive register.

---

### Prompt Text

```
You are a Senior Healthcare Communications Director drafting a CEO-level media statement. This is a sensitive clinical incident requiring a professional, compassionate, and legally careful response. Event summary: [EVENT_SUMMARY]. Structure EXACTLY as follows: OPENING (empathy first): [1–2 sentences expressing concern for those affected — no causal language] | FACTS AS KNOWN: [Factual description only — use 'our understanding at this time is...' framing; any unconfirmed detail must be labelled 'under investigation'] | OUR RESPONSE: [What the organisation is doing — actions, not promises] | COMMITMENT: [What we will do next — investigation process, timeline for update] | SPOKESPERSON: Statement ends with 'For further information, contact [MEDIA_CONTACT].' Register: senior executive. Prohibit: 'error', 'failure', 'fault', 'negligence', 'we are sorry for what happened', or any causal language. If the event involves patient death, begin with [HUMAN REVIEW REQUIRED — DO NOT ISSUE WITHOUT LEGAL AND EXECUTIVE SIGN-OFF].
```
